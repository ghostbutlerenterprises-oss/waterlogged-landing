Waterlogged — Design System

Field manual v1 · retro-nautical · est. Tampa Bay
01 — Foundations

Palette

An aged-chart palette: cream paper, ink-navy linework, one hot accent (buoy red) and a brass highlight. Use ink for type and strokes, buoy for primary action and emphasis, brass sparingly for highlights and tags. Color comes from ink and illustration, never from gradients.

02 — Foundations

Typography

Three voices. Fraunces (a wonky old-style serif) carries every headline and number — it's the single biggest reason this doesn't read generic. Work Sans keeps body copy quiet and legible. Courier Prime (typewriter) handles every label, tag, stat, and button — it reads as chart annotation and logbook entries.

03 — Components

Buttons & controls

Hard edges, 1.5px ink borders, and a hard offset shadow instead of soft blur — everything reads like a stamped ticket or chart inset. Toggles are rubber-stamp chips: dashed when off, inked solid when on.

Two core containers. The specimen plate (corner ticks, ruled border) frames illustration and hero imagery. The log card (numbered tab, ink border) holds entries — catches, features, list rows.

05 — Identity

Illustration & iconography

All art is ink line-work on paper — single-weight strokes, light cross-hatching for form, the look of an engraved field-guide plate. No filled silhouettes, no stock icon set. Species plates double as content (the catch) and decoration.

06 — Identity

Voice & motifs

Write like a guide's logbook, not a SaaS dashboard. Lean into nautical vernacular and concrete detail; keep labels in typewriter caps; sign things with coordinates and plate numbers.

Avoid (generic)
  • “Sign up for updates”
  • “Submit”
  • “Your dashboard”
  • “Catch added successfully”
  • “Settings”
Prefer (Waterlogged)
  • “Make your entry in the log”
  • “Sign the log”
  • “The log”
  • “Logged. — entry recorded”
  • “Ship's papers”
07 — Handoff

Tokens (drop into the app)

Same CSS custom properties power the landing page and these mocks. Reuse them in the Expo app (as a theme object) so web and native stay identical.
